The Volvo V40 is a premium compact hatchback manufactured from 2012 to 2019. Built on the Ford C1 platform (shared with Focus, Mazda 3 gen. 2), the V40 brought Volvo's safety pedigree to the C-segment. The top diesel variant, the D4, uses Volvo's own Drive-E D4204T16 2.0-litre four-cylinder engine – a modular, technically advanced unit combining a twin-scroll turbocharger, 2 000-bar piezo common-rail injection, and a full Euro 6 aftertreatment stack.

Twin-Scroll Turbo Air & Exhaust Flow
Twin-scroll design: The turbine housing is divided into two separate scroll passages, each fed by pairs of engine cylinders (1&4 and 2&3). This minimises pulse interference between cylinders, sustaining higher exhaust energy at low RPM and delivering boost earlier — contributing to the flat 400 Nm plateau from just 1 750 rpm.

Injection Pressure vs. Load
Piezo advantage: Piezo solenoids open/close in < 0.1 ms (vs. 0.3 ms for electromagnetic injectors), enabling multiple precisely-timed micro-injections per cycle. This reduces combustion knock, lowers NOₓ by up to 20 % and cuts soot formation by pre-conditioning the combustion chamber.

🔩 Pistons & Internal Components

Pistons

Cast aluminium alloy (AlSi12CuNiMg), steel-strapped for thermal expansion control. Bowl-in-piston design (toroidal re-entrant combustion bowl) promotes swirl and rapid fuel-air mixing. Gudgeon-pin offset to the thrust side to reduce piston slap.

Piston Rings (per piston)

1st compression ring: Rectangular, chrome-ceramic PVD coating, 1.5 mm.
2nd compression ring: Taper-faced, phosphate finish, 1.75 mm.
Oil scraper ring: Spring-loaded 3-piece steel, 2.0 mm. Total end gaps verified to 0.20–0.40 mm.

Connecting Rods

Fracture-split (cracked) forged steel (42CrMo4), H-beam cross-section. Length 156.5 mm (centre-to-centre). Big-end shell bearings: tri-metal (steel/aluminium/overlay). Torque to yield bolts, 30 Nm + 90°.

Crankshaft & Bearings

Forged steel, 5 main journals, fully counter-weighted. Journal diameter 57.982–58.000 mm. Crankshaft end-float 0.07–0.30 mm. Main bearing clearance 0.020–0.064 mm. Thrust washers at rear main bearing.

Cylinder Liner

Integral cast-iron bore (no separate liners). Plateau honed to Ra 0.4–0.8 µm. Bore tolerance H7 (+0.000/+0.021 mm). Cylinders sealed with MLS (multi-layer steel) head gasket, 1.37 mm nominal compressed thickness.

Cylinder Head & Valves

Aluminium alloy, cross-flow, 4 valves/cyl. Intake valve dia 30.6 mm; exhaust 27.0 mm. Hydraulic lash adjusters (no manual valve clearance). VVT (variable valve timing) on intake camshaft only, ± 40° authority, solenoid-actuated OCV.

Combustion bowl geometry: The toroidal re-entrant bowl creates a powerful squish zone at TDC, forcing the injected fuel spray along the bowl wall and inducing strong in-bowl swirl (swirl ratio ≈ 2.5). This maximises air-fuel mixing speed and enables high BMEP (≈ 20 bar) without excessive soot formation.
